1 Project Overview

This is it — your capstone. Over the last three years in the Web Design Pathway Program you have
learned how to build real websites from the ground up: HTML, CSS, Bootstrap, JavaScript, JSON,
jQuery, and Vue.js. You have built games, dynamic data-driven sites, and a full client website. Now
you bring it all together in one place that is entirely and uniquely yours.
Your senior portfolio website should do three things: showcase your best work, demonstrate your
growth as a developer and designer, and tell your story — who you are, where you&#39;ve been, and
where you&#39;re headed.
CAPSTONE STANDARD
This project should feel like the best work you have ever produced in this program. Effort, polish, and
intentionality are expected at every level — from your design choices down to your code comments.

2 Required Process

You must follow all five phases of the design process below, in order. Each phase produces work that
builds on the last — skipping steps will hurt your final result and your grade.
STEP 1
Research
• Find 2–3 real professional web portfolios and study them.
• For each: what you like about the layout, visuals, or UX; why it works; one thing you would do
differently.
• Save screenshots and notes — you will use these in your wireframes and presentation.

STEP 2
Planning — Wireframes
• Create both before writing any code:
• Low-Fidelity Wireframe — rough sketch of layout, sections, and navigation flow.
• High-Fidelity Wireframe — detailed mockup with colors, typography, spacing, and real
content.
• Tools: Figma, Adobe XD, Canva, or hand-drawn (photographed). Both wireframes must be
submitted.

Technical Requirements
Your site must demonstrate the full range of skills you have developed in this program. The following
are required — not optional.
REQUIREMENT DETAILS
JSON Data File Store your project data in a .json file. Your site must read from this file to

display content — not hardcoded HTML.

Vue.js or jQuery Use Vue or jQuery to dynamically render your project content from the JSON

file using the fetch protocol, as you did in Level 3.

GitHub Repository All code must be in a GitHub repo with a meaningful commit history showing

your progress over time.

GitHub Pages Your final site must be live and accessible via a GitHub Pages link.
Responsive Design The site must work on desktop, tablet, and mobile. Layouts should feel

intentionally designed for each — not just shrunk down.

5 Design &amp; Creativity
Design is a major focus of this project. Your site should be uniquely yours — it should not look like a
template, and it should not look like your classmates&#39; sites.
You are expected to:
• Use custom fonts and a personal color palette
• Create a distinct visual identity that feels personal and intentional
• Make thoughtful layout choices — not just a default Bootstrap grid
• Design your mobile layout intentionally — not just a smaller version of desktop
YOU MAY ALSO USE
CSS animations, GSAP, or other animation libraries. You may start from a template, but it must be
significantly customized so that it clearly reflects your own design vision. If your site looks like an
unmodified template, it will not score well on creativity.

6 AI Usage Policy &amp; Documentation
You are permitted to use AI tools such as ChatGPT, GitHub Copilot, or Claude to assist you.
However, AI use must be documented and reflected on — this is non-negotiable.
For every AI interaction you use, document:
• The exact prompt you entered
• What the AI returned
• How you used, modified, or rejected the output
• What you personally understand about what was generated
What to submit:
A document or dedicated page/section on your site with at least 3 specific examples of AI use, showing the
prompt, output, and your reflection.
